current research

PDF version of my CV

I am post-doctoral researcher working in the lab of Dr. Adam Famoso at Louisiana State University. The Famoso lab is part of the LSU AgCenter and runs one of the biggest public rice breeding programs in the Southern US. I am based at Cornell University as a visiting scholar in the Robbins Lab where I also work in close collaboration with the McCouch lab. The aim of my research is to determine optimal strategies for integrating new breeding technologies, mainly genome-wide markers and predictions, into the LSU progam. In order to do this, I am analyzing historical breeding data, running simulations, and leading proof-of-concept experiments within the breeding progam. As part of these efforts, I have designed and validated custom marker sets for program-wide genotyping, and established a breeding data management system.


past research

I completed my Ph.D. in 2019 at Cornell University in the Section of Plant Breeding and Genetics in the School of Integrative Plant Sciences. My Ph.D. advisor was Dr. Michael Mazourek, and my research project was focused on understanding and improving fruit quality traits in winter squash (Cucurbita moschata, Cucurbita maxima and Cucurbita pepo). My work included evaluating progress from several rounds of genomic selection, studying the transcriptome of developing fruit, and characterizing the genetic diversity of the USDA Cucurbita subsp. collection. My dissertation committee members were Dr. Jessica Rutkoski and Dr. Li Li.


research interests

There is a lot of excitement over integrating novel technologies and methods into breeding programs; however, the premature application of these technologies can do more harm than good. My interest lies in bridging the gap between new methods and their at-scale use in breeding programs. I am interested in applying the tools of genetics, statistics, bioinformatics, and practical thinking to enhance variety development. My ideal job would be in industry, preferably in the realm of vegetables or other specialty crops.
